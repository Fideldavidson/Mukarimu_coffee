Our Story

Mukarimu Coffee Estate began with a modest inheritance of 150 coffee trees in the heart of Meru County, Kenya. Founded by Charles Mutwiri Rintaugu, the estate grew from a family legacy into a thriving center of coffee excellence. Nestled on the eastern slopes of Mount Kenya, our farm benefits from rich volcanic soils and a climate ideal for cultivating high-grade coffee. Since our formal registration in 2005, we’ve expanded our operations while staying true to our roots— empowering youth, training farmers, and producing coffee that reflects the passion and resilience of our community. Every bean we harvest carries the story of growth, sustainability, and a commitment to quality that defines Mukarimu.

Our Services

Our estate goes beyond coffee farming by offering a full range of services designed to support upcoming and established growers. We provide professional training on modern coffee farming practices, helping farmers improve productivity and quality. In addition, we supply high-quality coffee seedlings to ensure strong, healthy plantations. We also engage in the sale of processed coffee, delivering well-prepared, carefully handled products to the market. Through these services, we aim to promote excellence and sustainability in the coffee industry.

AA GRADE

Kenya’s top grade, featuring large beans with high density. Renowned for vibrant acidity, full body, and complex fruity notes.

AB GRADE

A mix of slightly smaller AA and B beans. Offers balanced acidity, medium body, and consistent flavor — a popular export grade.

PB Grade

Peaberry beans — small, round, and formed solo in the cherry. Known for concentrated sweetness and a lively cup profile.
